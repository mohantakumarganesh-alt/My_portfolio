Effects of Agricultural Mechanization on Rural Employment: A Sociological Study of Rasgovindpur 
Block, Mayurbhanj District  
 
Abstract  
Agricultural mechanization has transformed traditional farming practices by increasing efficiency and 
productivity. However, its impact on rural employment remains a subject of sociological concern, especially 
in agrarian regions like Rasgovindpur Block of Mayurbhanj District, Odisha. This study examines how the 
adoption of modern agricultural machinery —such as tractors, harvesters, and threshers —affects 
employment opportunities, labour relations, and socio -econo mic conditions of rural workers. The research 
explores both the positive and negative consequences of mechanization, including labour displacement, skill 
transformation, and changing class dynamics. Using a mixed -method approach, the study highlights the 
complex relationship between technological advancement and rural livelihoods.  
 
Chapter 1: Introduction  
1.1 Background of the Study  
Agriculture has traditionally been labour -intensive in rural India. With the introduction of mechanization, 
farming practices have become more efficient, but the demand for manual labour has changed significantly.  
1.2 Statement of the Problem  
While mechanization increases productivity, it may reduce employment opportunities for agricultural 
labourers. There is limited localized research on its socio -economic effects in Rasgovindpur Block.  
1.3 Objectives of the Study  
 To examine the extent of agricultural mechanization in Rasgovindpur Block  
 To analyse its impact  on rural employment  
 To study changes in labour relations and wage patterns  
 To explore alternative livelihood opportunities  
1.4 Research Questions  
 How does mechanization affect employment in agriculture?  
 What changes occur in wage patterns and labour deman d? 
 How do rural workers adapt to mechanization?  
1.5 Significance of the Study  
This study contributes to rural sociology, development studies, and labour studies by examining the socio -
economic implications of mechanization.

Chapter 11: Conclusion and Suggestions  
Conclusion  
Agricultural mechanization in Rasgovindpur Block has led to significant changes in rural employment 
patterns. While it enhances efficiency, it also creates challenges for labourers, particular ly unskilled 
workers.  
Suggestions  
 Promote skill development programs  
 Encourage labour -intensive rural industries  
 Provide subsidies for small farmers  
 Strengthen employment schemes like MGNREGA
